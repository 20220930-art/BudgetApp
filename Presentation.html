1. Setup and Installation

Prerequisites

  • Node.js (v18 or higher)
  • PostgreSQL (v15 or higher)
  • npm (comes with Node.js)

Steps

1 Create a PostgreSQL database named budgetapp
2 Open the .env file and update the database connection string:
DATABASE_URL=postgresql://username:password@localhost:5432/budgetapp
3 Open a terminal in the project folder and run:
npm install
4 Start the server:
npm start
5 Open your browser and go to http://localhost:3000
The database tables are created automatically when the server starts for the first time. Default categories (Salary, Freelance, Food, Transport, Shopping, Bills, Entertainment, Health, Other) are added when you register.

2. Creating an Account

1 Open the app in your browser. You will see the login page.
2 Click "Don't have an account? Sign up" at the bottom.
3 Enter your name, email, and a password.
4 Click "Sign Up". You will be redirected to the Dashboard automatically.

3. Logging In

1 Enter your email and password on the login page.
2 Click "Login".
3 If the credentials are correct, you will be taken to the Dashboard.
If you enter a wrong email or password, a red error message will appear below the form.

4. Dashboard

The Dashboard is the main screen you see after logging in. It shows:

CardDescription
BalanceYour total income minus total expenses
IncomeTotal money you earned
ExpensesTotal money you spent
Active BudgetsNumber of budgets currently being tracked
Active GoalsNumber of savings goals in progress

Below the cards, you can see your 5 most recent transactions.

5. Managing Transactions

Adding a Transaction

1 Click "Transactions" in the navigation bar.
2 Click the "+ Add Transaction" button at the top.
3 Fill in the form:
  • Type: Choose "Income" or "Expense"
  • Category: Select a category (changes based on type)
  • Amount: Enter the amount in EGP
  • Date: Pick the date
  • Description: Optional note about the transaction
4 Click "Save".

Editing a Transaction

1 Find the transaction in the table.
2 Click the "Edit" button next to it.
3 The form will appear pre-filled with the transaction data. Make your changes and click "Save".

Deleting a Transaction

1 Click the "Delete" button next to the transaction.
2 Confirm the deletion in the popup.

6. Creating and Tracking Budgets

Creating a Budget

1 Click "Budgets" in the navigation bar.
2 Click "+ Create Budget".
3 Fill in the form:
  • Category: Which expense category to track
  • Amount: Your monthly spending limit in EGP
  • Month/Year: Which month this budget applies to
  • Alert %: At what percentage you want to be warned (default 80%)
4 Click "Save".

Budget Progress

Each budget shows a progress bar:

  • Green = spending is under control
  • Orange = nearing your alert threshold
  • Red = you exceeded the budget

7. Budget Alerts

When you add an expense, the system automatically checks if you are close to or over your budget for that category:

StatusWhat Happens
Under thresholdNothing happens, progress bar stays green
Near limit (e.g. 80%+)Orange warning notification is created
ExceededRed alert notification is created
You can see alerts in the Notifications page. The badge on "Notifications" in the navbar shows how many unread alerts you have.

8. Savings Goals

Creating a Goal

1 Click "Goals" in the navigation bar.
2 Click "+ New Goal".
3 Enter a name, target amount, and optional deadline.
4 Click "Save". The goal appears with a 0% progress bar.

Adding Money to a Goal

1 Find the goal card.
2 Enter an amount in the small input field.
3 Click "Add Money".
4 The progress bar updates. When you reach the target, a congratulations message appears and the goal is marked as completed.

9. Reports and Analytics

1 Click "Reports" in the navigation bar.
2 Optionally select a date range (From and To).
3 Click "Generate".

The report shows:

  • Total Income / Total Expenses / Savings Rate - summary cards at the top
  • Spending by Category - table showing how much you spent per category
  • Monthly Trend - income and expenses broken down by month
  • Top expense category - which category you spend the most on

10. Notifications

1 Click "Notifications" in the navigation bar (shows a red badge with unread count).
2 You will see a list of all budget alerts with timestamps.
3 Click "Mark all as read" to clear the badge counter.
